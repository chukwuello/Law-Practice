# 01 – Pleadings

## Definition

**Pleadings**: A document in which a party to a civil proceeding sets forth or responds to allegations, claims, or defenses.  
— *Black's Law Dictionary*

In **Adeniji v. Tukur (2025) 19 NWLR (Pt. 2019) 145**, the court held:

> *"Pleadings in our procedural rules clearly set out claims, defences, and legal issues parties intend to resolve in court."*

The court further stated:

> *"The statement of claim and the statement of defence are the pleadings of the parties to the action."*

## What Constitutes Pleading?

- Statement of Claim  
- Statement of Defence  
- Reply  
- Counter-Claim  
- Set-off  
- Defence to Counter-Claim  
- Petition  
- Answer to Petition

## Facts vs. Evidence in Pleadings

**Fact** (Black's Law Dictionary):  
*"A thing done; an action performed or an incident transpiring; an event or circumstance; an actual occurrence…"*

**Evidence Act, Section 258(1)**:  
Fact includes:
- (a) Anything capable of being perceived by the senses; and  
- (b) Any mental condition of which a person is conscious.

**Fact-in-Issue**: Facts that must be proved by the claimant or defendant to succeed.

### Example: Libel Suit – Facts in Issue
1. The defendant published the defamatory statement in permanent form.
2. The statement was false.
3. The statement referred to the plaintiff.
4. The statement conveyed a defamatory meaning.
5. The statement was defamatory of the plaintiff.
