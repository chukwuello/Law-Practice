# 04 – Key Differences: Pleadings vs. Evidence

| **Pleadings** | **Evidence** |
|--------------|-------------|
| Written statements of **material facts** | Means by which those facts are **proved** |
| Filed **before trial** | Presented **during trial** |
| Governed by **Rules of Court** | Governed by **Evidence Act** |
| Contain **facts only** | Contain **testimony, documents, objects** |
| Define the **scope of dispute** | Determine **proof** of the dispute |

---

## Illustration

**Statement of Claim**:
> *"The Claimant states that on 1st April 2026, he bought 10 bags of Cement from the Defendant."*

This is a **fact pleaded** – it belongs in pleadings.

If the Claimant later tenders a **receipt dated 1st April 2026**, that receipt is **evidence**.

---

## Common Drafting Error

❌ **Wrong**:  
*"The claimant paid ₦500,000 to the defendant **as shown in the receipt dated 1 January 2024**."*  
(The phrase "as shown in the receipt" is evidence, not a fact.)

✅ **Correct**:  
*"The claimant paid ₦500,000 to the defendant on 1 January 2024."*
