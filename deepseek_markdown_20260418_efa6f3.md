# 02 – Types of Pleadings

## 1. Statement of Claim
A written statement by which a Claimant sets out **material facts** constituting the cause of action and the reliefs sought.

**Contents**:
- Facts of the case
- Injury suffered
- Remedies sought

## 2. Statement of Defence
A written statement by which a defendant responds to the Claimant’s statement of claim by **admitting, denying, or explaining** the allegations.

**Contents**:
- Grounds of defence
- Facts supporting the defendant’s case

## 3. Reply (Reply to Statement of Defence)
Filed by the Claimant to answer **new issues of fact** raised in the defence.

> ❓ Is a reply always necessary? No – only if new matters are raised.

## 4. Counterclaim
A statement by which the defendant asserts a **separate and independent claim** against the Claimant within the same action.

- Treated as a separate suit.
- Defendant becomes the Claimant in that regard.

## 5. Set-Off
Applies **solely to monetary claims**. It is a monetary claim by the defendant that reduces or extinguishes the Claimant’s claim.

- If the set-off exceeds the claim, judgment may be entered for the balance in favour of the Claimant.

## 6. Defence to Counterclaim
Filed by the Claimant in response to the defendant’s counterclaim.

## 7. Counter-Claimant’s Reply to Defence to Counterclaim
A further reply filed **with leave of court**.

## 8. Petition
Used in:
- Matrimonial causes (*Section 54(1) Matrimonial Causes Act*)
- Election matters (*Section 134 Electoral Act 2026*)
- Company winding up (*Section 578 CAMA*)

**Petition and Answer** are equivalent to Statement of Claim and Defence respectively.
