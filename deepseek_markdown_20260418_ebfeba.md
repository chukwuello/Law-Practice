# 03 – Evidence

## Definition

**Evidence**: Something (including testimony, documents, and tangible objects) that tends to prove or disprove the existence of an alleged fact.  
— *Black's Law Dictionary*

In **Tukur v. Uba (2013) 4 NWLR (Pt. 1343) 90**:

> *"Evidence, broadly defined, is the means from which an inference may logically be drawn as to the existence of a fact."*

**Section 1, Evidence Act**:  
Evidence may be given of the existence or non‑existence of every **fact in issue** and of such other facts declared relevant.

---

## Classification of Evidence

### Oral Evidence
Testimony given in person by a witness.  
📖 *Section 125, Evidence Act*

### Real Evidence
Tangible objects or things you can see and touch tendered before the court.  
*Examples: weapon, damaged property*

### Documentary Evidence
Written or recorded materials used to prove facts.  
📖 *Section 83, Evidence Act*  
*Examples: contracts, receipts*

### Electronic Evidence
Evidence stored or transmitted electronically.  
📖 *Section 84, Evidence Act*  
*Examples: emails, WhatsApp messages*

### Direct Evidence
Evidence that directly proves a fact without inference.  
📖 *Section 126, Evidence Act*  
*Example: Eyewitness testimony*

### Circumstantial Evidence
Evidence that suggests a fact indirectly through inference.

### Primary Evidence
Original documents or first‑hand evidence.  
*Example: Original receipt*

### Secondary Evidence
Copies or reproductions of original evidence.  
*Examples: photocopies, scanned documents*

## Query: Proving Intangible Assets

> If shares are intangible assets, how do you prove ownership?

Answer: Through documentary evidence such as **share certificates, CSCS statements, or company register extracts**.
