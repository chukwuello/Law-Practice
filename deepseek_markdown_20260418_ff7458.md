# 06 – Legal Authorities

## Case Law

### Adeniji v. Tukur (2025) 19 NWLR (Pt. 2019) 145
- Pleadings set out claims, defences, and issues for resolution.

### Ijomah v. Molokwu (2025) 21 NWLR (Pt. 2023) 145
- Evidence at variance with pleadings is inadmissible and goes to no issue.

### Aghadiuno v. Onubogu (1998) 5 NWLR (Pt. 548) 16
- Averments in pleadings are **not evidence**.

### Tukur v. Uba (2013) 4 NWLR (Pt. 1343) 90
- Evidence is the means from which inferences of fact are drawn.

## Statutory Provisions

### Evidence Act, 2011
- **Section 1**: Evidence may be given only of facts in issue or relevant facts.
- **Section 83**: Documentary evidence.
- **Section 84**: Electronic evidence.
- **Section 125**: Oral evidence.
- **Section 126**: Direct evidence.
- **Section 258(1)**: Definition of "fact".

### FCT High Court Civil Procedure Rules, Order 15 Rule 2

> *"Every pleading shall contain a statement in a summary form of the material facts on which the party pleading relies for his claim or defence, as the case may be, **but not the evidence** by which they are to be proved."*
