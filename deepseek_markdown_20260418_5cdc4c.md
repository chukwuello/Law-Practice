## 📌 Expected Outcomes

By the end of this resource, you should be able to:

- Understand the concept of pleadings and their role in civil litigation.
- Identify and distinguish different types of pleadings.
- Separate material facts from evidence.
- Draft basic pleadings correctly without conflating facts with evidence.
- Apply procedural rules governing pleadings.
- Avoid common drafting errors.

## ⚖️ Core Principle

> **Facts are pleaded; evidence is proved.**  
> Pleadings contain *material facts* only—never the evidence by which they are to be proved.
