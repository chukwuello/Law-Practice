# 05 – Similarities & Relationship

## Facts Pleaded Must Be Supported by Evidence

**Example**:
- Pleading: *"I graduated from GSS Garki in 2005."*
- At trial: Tendered **Certified True Copy of First School Leaving Certificate**.

## Facts Not Backed by Evidence Are Deemed Abandoned

If the party fails to tender the certificate, the pleaded fact is abandoned and the court will not act on it.

## Evidence Must Not Contradict Pleadings

> *"Parties are bound by their pleadings and evidence led on a fact not pleaded, or which is at variance with pleading, is inadmissible and goes to no issue. This is so even if the evidence is given under cross‑examination."*  
— **Ijomah v. Molokwu (2025) 21 NWLR (Pt. 2023) 145**

**Example**:
- Pleading: *"I graduated from GSS Garki in 2005."*
- Evidence tendered: Certificate from **GSS Asokoro** dated **1999** → **Inadmissible**.

## Evidence Led on a Fact Not Pleaded Goes to No Issue

If a fact was not pleaded, evidence to prove it cannot be admitted.

## Cross‑Examination

A witness may be cross‑examined based on both **pleadings and evidence** to test consistency.
